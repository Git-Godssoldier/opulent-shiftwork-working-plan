AI LABORER ONBOARDING TEMPLATE
01  —  MARKETING
Discovery Questions
AREA
Marketing  —  the agents that fill the funnel
BUILDS
Social posts, email newsletters, review requests, referral activation
HOW TO USE
This is a template. The system fills the brackets, then a business owner answers in plain words.
Answer in plain language. Bullet points are fine. The honest, messy parts make the agents smarter.
HOW THE BRACKETS WORK
[Business Name]  A capitalized item is a value the system drops in. Swap it for the real detail.
[list the channels they use]  An instruction tells the system to write custom examples for this business. Replace it.
Plain text with no brackets  stays the same for every business.
A
BRAND & AUDIENCE
M01   In a few words, how should [Business Name] sound?
Pick the feeling, not the exact words. [suggest 2-3 voice traits that fit their industry, like warm, expert, or bold]. If they have a known credential or angle, name it [insert their main differentiator or credential] because it shapes the voice.
M02   Who are you trying to reach with your marketing?
Describe them like a real person, not a category. [add a profile hint that fits their business, like life stage, job, or location]. Who do you most want walking in the door?
M03   What 3 to 5 things should [Business Name] be known for talking about?
These are your pillars. [list 3-5 content themes common in their industry]. They become the topics the content agent posts about.
B
CHANNELS & POSTING  —  feeds the Social Media agent
M04   Where do you post now, and where do you wish you posted?
[list the platforms common in their industry, like Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, or Google Business]. Give a realistic number of posts per week for each.
M05   When you go quiet on a channel, what's the reason?
No time? Not sure what to say? Just forget? This gap is exactly what the Social Media agent fills.
C
EMAIL  —  feeds the Email Newsletter agent
M06   Do you email your customer list today? If so, what do you send and how often?
Newsletters, tips, promos, or nothing at all. Be honest. [name an email tool they likely use, if known]. The Email agent picks up from here.
M07   How big is your email list, and how clean is it?
A rough number is fine. Small or messy is still a useful answer.
D
REVIEWS & REFERRALS  —  feeds the Review Request agent
M08   How and when do you ask happy customers for a review today?
After the sale? After the job is done? Never? [name the review sites that matter most for their business, like Google, Yelp, or an industry site]. The Review agent asks at the right moment.
M09   What happens today when someone sends you a referral?
Is there a process, a thank-you, or a reward? Or does it just happen by luck? [note any referral source that matters to their business].
E
OFFERS & HOOKS
M10   Do you have any free offer, guide, or promo that pulls new people in?
A quote, a checklist, a discount, a quick consult. If you don't have one, say so. That is a gap worth filling.
Every answer here trains a Marketing agent. The clearer the answer, the less guessing build day needs.
